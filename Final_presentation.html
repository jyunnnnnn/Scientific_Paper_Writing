Defining the Gap: The Pivot

"Hardware research is limited"

Through "However," the author shifts focus to specific challenges.

Challenge 01

Incompatibility

Element-wise ops vs. Tensor Core

The Conflict

Tensor Cores are built for reduction. Mamba's element-wise ops don't need reduction, leading to massive inefficiency.

Evidence

"...1/16 normalized speed."

Strategy: Using numbers to prove inefficiency.

Challenge 02

Area Overhead

Wasted silicon area

The Conflict

Specialized units for nonlinear functions (like Exp) occupy too much space but are not always used.

Evidence

"...occupies >30% of the PE area."

Strategy: Criticizing resource waste.

Hover to Expand
Challenge 03

Memory Bottleneck

Existing methods fail

The Conflict

High memory access but limited data reuse makes traditional 'Tiling' optimization impossible.

Evidence

"...it is useless to apply existed methods."

Strategy: Using the strong word "Useless".

Hover to Expand

Strategy Summary: Move 3

The author goes beyond simply stating problems. They use Negative Evaluation words like "Incompatibility" and "Useless" to intensify the tone. In academic writing, this is called Problematization, aiming to create an indispensable need for the proposed solution.
